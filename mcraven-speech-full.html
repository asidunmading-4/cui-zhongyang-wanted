🪖 If You Want to Change the World

Admiral William H. McRaven
UT Austin 2014 毕业演讲 · 原文全文双语对照
"Make Your Bed" — 整理你的床铺

President Powers, Provost Fenves, Deans, members of the faculty, family and friends and most importantly, the class of 2014. Congratulations on your achievement.
Powers 校长、Fenves 教务长、各位院长、教职员工、家属和朋友们，最重要的是——2014届的毕业生们，祝贺你们。
It's been almost 37 years to the day that I graduated from UT. I remember a lot of things about that day. I remember I had throbbing headache from a party the night before. I remember I had a serious girlfriend, whom I later married — that's important to remember by the way — and I remember that I was getting commissioned in the Navy that day.
我毕业已经快37年了。我记得那天很多事情：记得前一天晚上聚会后头痛欲裂；记得当时有一个认真的女朋友，后来我娶了她——这点很重要；还有我记得那天我要被任命为海军军官。
But of all the things I remember, I don't have a clue who the commencement speaker was that evening, and I certainly don't remember anything they said. So, acknowledging that fact, if I can't make this commencement speech memorable, I will at least try to make it short.
但在所有我记住的事情中，我完全不记得那天晚上是谁做了毕业典礼演讲，也完全不记得他们说了什么。所以考虑到这一点，如果我不能让这个演讲令人难忘，我至少会尽量让它简短。
The University's slogan is, "What starts here changes the world." I have to admit — I kinda like it. Tonight there are almost 8,000 students graduating from UT. That great paragon of analytical rigor, Ask.Com, says that the average American will meet 10,000 people in their lifetime.
大学的口号是："从这里开始，改变世界"。我得承认，我挺喜欢的。今晚有将近8000名学生从UT毕业。那个以严谨分析著称的Ask.com说，普通美国人一生会见到10000人。
But, if every one of you changed the lives of just 10 people — and each one of those folks changed the lives of another 10 people — just 10 — then in five generations — 125 years — the class of 2014 will have changed the lives of 800 million people. Over twice the population of the United States. Go one more generation and you can change the entire population of the world — eight billion people.
但如果在座的每一位都仅仅改变10个人的生活，而这10个人中的每位又改变另外10个人的生活——那么在五代之后、125年，2014届将改变8亿人的生活。超过美国人口的两倍。再下一代，你就可以改变全世界80亿人。
If you think it's hard to change the lives of 10 people forever, you're wrong. I saw it happen every day in Iraq and Afghanistan: A young Army officer makes a decision to go left instead of right down a road in Baghdad and the 10 soldiers in his squad are saved from close-in ambush. In Kandahar province, a non-commissioned officer senses something isn't right and directs the infantry platoon away from a 500-pound IED, saving the lives of a dozen soldiers.
如果你认为改变10个人的生活很难，那你就错了。我在伊拉克和阿富汗每天都看到这种情况发生：一位年轻陆军军官决定在巴格达的一条路上左转而不是右转，他身边的10名士兵从近距离伏击中获救。在坎大哈省，一名士官感觉到有什么不对劲，引导步兵排避开一枚500磅的简易爆炸装置，挽救了十多名士兵的生命。
Not only were these soldiers saved by one person's decisions, but their children yet unborn were also saved. Generations were saved by one decision, by one person. Changing the world can happen anywhere and anyone can do it.
这些士兵不仅因为一个人的决定而获救，他们未出生的孩子也得救了。一代又一代的人因为一个决定、一个人而获救。改变世界可以在任何地方发生，任何人都可以做到。
But if you will humor this old sailor for just a moment, I have a few suggestions that may help you on your way. While these lessons were learned during my time in the military, I can assure you that it matters not whether you ever served a day in uniform. It matters not your gender, ethnic or religious background, orientation or social status. Our struggles are similar, and the lessons to overcome them apply equally to all.
但如果你能容忍这个老水手一会儿，我有几个建议可能对你有帮助。虽然这些经验是在我从军期间学到的，但我可以向你保证：无论你是否服役一天，无论性别、种族、宗教背景、取向或社会地位如何，这些建议都适用。我们的挣扎是相似的，克服它们的方法对所有人都同样适用。
I have been a Navy SEAL for 36 years. But it all began when I left UT for Basic SEAL training in Coronado, California. Basic SEAL training is six months of long torturous runs in the soft sand, midnight swims in the cold water off San Diego, obstacle courses, unending calisthenics, days without sleep and always being cold, wet and miserable. It is six months of being constantly harassed by professionally trained warriors who seek to find the weak of mind and body and eliminate them.
我做海豹突击队队员已经36年了。一切都始于我离开UT去加州科罗纳多进行基础海豹突击队训练。基础训练为期六个月：漫长而痛苦的软沙长跑、圣地亚哥冰冷海水中的午夜游泳、障碍训练、无休止的体能训练、几天不睡觉，总是又冷又湿又难受。这是六个月不断被职业军人骚扰，他们试图找出精神和身体上的弱者并将他们淘汰。
But the training also seeks to find those who can lead in an environment of constant stress, chaos, failure and hardships. To me basic SEAL training was a lifetime of challenges crammed into six months. So here are the 10 lessons I learned from basic SEAL training that hopefully will be of value to you.
但训练也会找出那些能在持续压力、混乱、失败和困难环境中领导的学生。对我来说，基础海豹训练是一生挑战的浓缩，塞进六个月。所以这里是我学到的10个教训，希望对你们有价值。

✦ ✦ ✦

1Make Your Bed · 整理你的床铺
Every morning in basic SEAL training, my instructors, who at the time were all Vietnam veterans, would show up in my barracks room and the first thing they would inspect was your bed. If you did it right, the corners would be square, the covers pulled tight, the pillow centered just under the headboard and the extra blanket folded neatly at the foot of the rack — that's Navy talk for bed.

It was a simple task — mundane at best. But every morning we were required to make our bed to perfection. It seemed a little ridiculous at the time, particularly in light of the fact that we were aspiring to be real warriors, tough battle-hardened SEALs, but the wisdom of this simple act has been proven to me many times over.

If you make your bed every morning you will have accomplished the first task of the day. It will give you a small sense of pride, and it will encourage you to do another task and another and another. By the end of the day, that one task completed will have turned into many tasks completed. Making your bed will also reinforce the fact that little things in life matter. If you can't do the little things right, you will never do the big things right.

And if by chance you have a miserable day, you will come home to a bed that is made — that you made — and a made bed gives you encouragement that tomorrow will be better.

If you want to change the world, start off by making your bed.
每天早上教官们（当时都是越战老兵）会出现在我的营房，首先检查床铺。如果叠对了，角落方正，床单拉紧，枕头居中放在床头板下，备用毯子整齐地叠在床尾——这是海军对"床"的说法。

这是一项简单的任务——平淡无奇。但每天早上我们都必须把床整理得完美无缺。当时看来有点荒谬，特别是我们渴望成为真正的战士、坚韧的海豹突击队员。但这简单行为中的智慧已被我多次证明。

每天整理床铺 = 完成当天第一个任务 → 自豪感 → 鼓励完成下一个、再下一个。到一天结束时，一项完成的任务变成许多完成的任务。整理床铺也强化一个事实：生活中的小事很重要。

如果哪天过得很糟，回到家有一张自己整好的床——它会给你鼓励，明天会更好。

如果你想改变世界，从整理你的床铺开始。
2Find Someone to Help You Paddle · 找人帮你一起划船
During SEAL training the students are broken down into boat crews. Each crew is seven students — three on each side of a small rubber boat and one coxswain to help guide the dingy. Every day your boat crew forms up on the beach and is instructed to get through the surf zone and paddle several miles down the coast.

In winter, the surf off San Diego can get to be 8 to 10 feet high and it is exceedingly difficult to paddle through the plunging surf unless everyone digs in. Every paddle must be synchronized to the stroke count of the coxswain. Everyone must exert equal effort or the boat will turn against the wave and be unceremoniously tossed back on the beach.

For the boat to make it to its destination, everyone must paddle. You can't change the world alone — you will need some help — and to truly get from your starting point to your destination takes friends, colleagues, the good will of strangers and a strong coxswain to guide them.

If you want to change the world, find someone to help you paddle.
训练中学生被分成橡皮艇小组。每组7人——小船两侧各3人，一人掌舵引导方向。每天小组在海滩集合，穿过冲浪带沿海岸划行数英里。

冬天，圣地亚哥海浪可达8-10英尺高。除非每个人都全力以赴，否则很难划破猛烈的海浪。每一次划桨都必须与舵手的节奏同步。每个人都必须付出同等努力，否则船会被海浪打翻，狼狈地拍回沙滩。

船要到达目的地，每个人都必须划船。你不能独自改变世界——你需要帮助——朋友、同事、陌生人的善意，和一个强有力的舵手来引导。

如果你想改变世界，找一个人帮你划船。
3Measure by Heart, Not Flippers · 看内心大小，不是脚蹼大小
Over a few weeks of difficult training my SEAL class, which started with 150 men, was down to just 35. There were now six boat crews of seven men each. I was in the boat with the tall guys, but the best boat crew we had was made up of the little guys — the munchkin crew we called them — no one was over about five-foot-five.

The munchkin crew had one American Indian, one African American, one Polish American, one Greek American, one Italian American, and two tough kids from the midwest. They out-paddled, out-ran and out-swam all the other boat crews. The big men in other crews would always make fun of the tiny little flippers the munchkins put on prior to every swim. But somehow these little guys always had the last laugh — swimming faster than everyone and reaching the shore long before us.

SEAL training was a great equalizer. Nothing mattered but your will to succeed. Not your color, not your ethnic background, not your education and not your social status.

If you want to change the world, measure a person by the size of their heart, not the size of their flippers.
经过几周艰苦训练，我的班级从150人减少到只剩35人。现在有六个小艇组，每组七人。我在高个子组，但我们最好的小艇组是矮个子组成的——我们叫他们"小矮人组"，没有人超过五英尺五寸。

小矮人组有一名美洲印第安人、一名非裔美国人、一名波兰裔美国人、一名希腊裔美国人、一名意大利裔美国人，还有两个来自中西部的坚韧孩子。他们的划船、跑步、游泳碾压了所有人。大块头们总是嘲笑小矮人们在每次游泳前套上的小小脚蹼。但这些小家伙总是笑到最后，比所有人都游得更快，最先到达岸边。

海豹训练是一个伟大的平等者。什么都不重要，只看你有没意愿成功。不看肤色、种族背景、教育程度或社会地位。

如果你想改变世界，用内心的大小衡量一个人，而不是脚蹼的大小。
4Get Over Being a Sugar Cookie · 接受成为"糖霜饼干"
Several times a week, the instructors would line up the class and do a uniform inspection. It was exceptionally thorough. Your hat had to be perfectly starched, your uniform immaculately pressed and your belt buckle shiny and void of any smudges. But it seemed that no matter how much effort you put in — it just wasn't good enough. The instructors would find something wrong.

For failing inspection, you ran fully clothed into the surf zone, then wet from head to toe, rolled around on the beach until every part of your body was covered with sand. The effect was known as a "sugar cookie." You stayed in that uniform cold, wet and sandy all day.

There were many students who couldn't accept that all their effort was in vain. That no matter how hard they tried, it was unappreciated. Those students didn't make it through training. They didn't understand the purpose. You were never going to succeed. You were never going to have a perfect uniform.

Sometimes no matter how well you prepare or how well you perform you still end up as a sugar cookie. It's just the way life is sometimes.

If you want to change the world, get over being a sugar cookie and keep moving forward.
每周几次，教官让学生排队进行着装检查。非常彻底：帽子必须浆硬完美，军服熨烫一尘不染，皮带扣锃亮无污渍。但似乎无论你多努力——都不够好。教官总能挑出毛病。

检查不合格 → 全身穿着衣服跑进冲浪带 → 打湿后在沙滩上滚一圈直到全身沾满沙子。这种效果叫"糖霜饼干"。一整天穿着又冷又湿满是沙子的军服。

很多学生无法接受努力白费的事实。不管多努力都得不到赏识。这些人没能通过训练。他们不明白训练目的：你永远不可能成功，永远不可能有一套完美的制服。

有时候，不管你准备得多好、表现多好，你还是一块糖霜饼干。生活有时就是这样。

如果你想改变世界，接受成为糖霜饼干，继续前进。
5Don't Be Afraid of the Circuses · 别怕"马戏团"
Every day during training you were challenged with multiple physical events — long runs, long swims, obstacle courses, hours of calisthenics — designed to test your mettle. Every event had standards — times you had to meet. If you failed to meet those standards your name went on a list, and at the end of the day those on the list were invited to a "circus."

A circus was two hours of additional calisthenics designed to wear you down, break your spirit, force you to quit. No one wanted a circus. A circus meant that for that day you didn't measure up. More fatigue meant the following day would be more difficult, and more circuses were likely.

But at some time during SEAL training, everyone — everyone — made the circus list. But an interesting thing happened to those constantly on the list. Over time those students got stronger and stronger. The pain of circuses built inner strength, built physical resiliency.

Life is filled with circuses. You will fail. You will likely fail often. It will be painful. It will be discouraging. At times it will test you to your very core.

If you want to change the world, don't be afraid of the circuses.
每天训练面临多项体能挑战——长跑、长距离游泳、障碍场、数小时体能训练——考验意志力。每项都有标准时间。不达标？名字上名单。一天结束时名单上的人被邀请参加"马戏团"。

"马戏团"是两小时额外体能训练，旨在让你疲惫不堪、摧毁精神、迫使你放弃。没人想要马戏团——意味着当天不合格。更多疲劳意味着第二天更困难，更多马戏团随之而来。

但在海豹训练期间某个时刻，每个人都会进马戏团名单。有趣的是那些常驻名单的人越来越强壮了。马戏团的痛苦培养了内在力量和身体韧性。

生活中充满马戏团。你会失败，很可能经常失败。它会痛苦、令人沮丧、有时考验你的核心。

如果你想改变世界，不要害怕马戏团。
6Slide Down Obstacle Head First · 头朝下冲过障碍
At least twice a week, trainees were required to run the obstacle course. It contained 25 obstacles including a 10-foot high wall, a 30-foot cargo net, barbed wire crawl, etc. But the most challenging obstacle was the "Slide for Life." A three-level 30-foot tower at one end, a one-level tower at the other, with a 200-foot rope between them.

You climbed the tower, grabbed the rope, swung underneath and pulled yourself hand-over-hand across. The record had stood for years when my class began in 1977. It seemed unbeatable — until one day, a student decided to go down head first.

Instead of swinging underneath the rope and inching his way down, he bravely mounted the TOP of the rope and thrust himself forward. It was dangerous — seemingly foolish, fraught with risk. Failure could mean injury and being dropped from training. Without hesitation he slid perilously fast. Instead of several minutes, it took half that time and he broke the record.

If you want to change the world, sometimes you have to slide down the obstacle head first.
每周至少跑两次障碍场。25个障碍物：10英尺高墙、30英尺货网、铁丝网匍匐前进等。最具挑战性的是"生命滑索"：一端三层30英尺塔，另一端单层塔，中间200英尺绳索。

爬上塔顶抓住绳子，在下方摆动双手交替拉过去。这个纪录在我1977年入学时已保持多年，似乎无人能破——直到有一天，一个学员决定头朝下滑下去。

他没有在绳子下面摆动一寸一寸爬，而是勇敢地爬上绳子的顶端向前冲刺。危险动作——看似愚蠢充满风险。失败意味着受伤退训。他毫不犹豫快速滑下，用一半时间破了纪录。

如果你想改变世界，有时候你必须头朝下冲过障碍。
7Don't Back Down from the Sharks · 不向大白鲨低头
During land warfare phase, students are flown to San Clemente Island off the coast of San Diego. The waters there are a breeding ground for great white sharks. One required swim is the night swim.

Before swimming, instructors joyfully brief trainees on all species of sharks inhabiting those waters. They assure you no student has ever been eaten by a shark — at least not recently. But you are also taught: if a shark begins to circle your position, stand your ground. Do not swim away. Do not act afraid. And if the shark, hungry for a midnight snack, darts towards you — summon all your strength and punch him in the snout, and he will turn and swim away.

There are a lot of sharks in this world. If you hope to complete the swim you will have to deal with them.

So, if you want to change the world, don't back down from the sharks.
陆战阶段，学员飞往圣地亚哥海岸外的圣克莱门特岛。水域是大白鲨繁殖地。必须完成一系列游泳，其中一次是夜游。

游前，教官兴高采烈地介绍所有鲨鱼种类。他们保证没有学生被鲨鱼吃过——至少最近没有。但你也被教导：鲨鱼围着你转？站稳别跑别怕。鲨鱼冲过来？调动全部力量击打它的鼻子，它就会转身游走。

世界上有很多鲨鱼。想完成游泳就必须学会与它们打交道。

如果你想改变世界，不要向大白鲨低头。
8Be Your Best in the Darkest Moment · 在最黑暗时刻做到最好
As Navy SEALs, one job is underwater attacks against enemy shipping. We practiced extensively during training. A pair of SEAL divers is dropped outside an enemy harbor, swims well over two miles underwater using only a depth gauge and compass to reach the target.

Even deep below surface, some light comes through. It is comforting to know open water is above. But as you approach the ship tied to a pier, light begins to fade. Steel blocks moonlight, street lamps, all ambient light.

To succeed you must swim under the ship and find the keel — centerline and deepest part. That's your objective. But the keel is also the darkest part — cannot see your hand in front of face, machinery noise deafening, easy to get disoriented and fail.

Every SEAL knows that under the keel, at the darkest moment, is when you must be calm, composed — when all tactical skills, physical power and inner strength must be brought to bear.

If you want to change the world, you must be your very best in the darkest moment.
作为海豹突击队，任务之一是对敌方船只进行水下攻击。我们广泛练习这项技术：一对潜水员在敌方港外放下，仅靠深度计和指南针在水下游两英里以上到达目标。

即使深处水面以下也有光线透过来，知道上方有开阔水域让人安心。但当接近船只时光线开始变暗：钢结构挡住月光、路灯、所有环境光。

要成功必须在船底找到龙骨（中线和最深部分）。龙骨也是最暗的地方——伸手不见五指，机器噪音震耳欲聋，容易迷失方向失败。

每个海豹都知道：龙骨之下，任务最暗时刻，必须冷静沉着——所有战术技能、体力和内在力量都必须发挥出来。

如果你想改变世界，在最黑暗时刻你必须做到最好。
9Start Singing When Up to Your Neck in Mud · 陷泥潭时开始唱歌
The ninth week is called "Hell Week." Six days of no sleep, constant harassment, and one special day at the Mud Flats between San Diego and Tijuana — swampy terrain where mud engulfs you.

On Wednesday of Hell Week, you paddle to the mud flats and spend 15 hours trying to survive freezing cold mud, howling wind and incessant pressure to quit from instructors. As the sun set that Wednesday evening, having committed some egregious infraction, my class was ordered into the mud. The mud consumed each man till nothing visible but our heads.

Instructors told us we could leave if just five men quit — just five men. Looking around, some students were about to give up. Still over eight hours till sunrise — more bone-chilling cold. Chattering teeth and shivering moans so loud it was hard to hear anything.

Then, one voice began to echo through the night, one voice raised in song. Terribly out of tune, but sung with great enthusiasm. One voice became two, two became three, before long everyone was singing. Instructors threatened more time in mud if we kept singing, but singing persisted. Somehow the mud seemed warmer, wind tamer, dawn not so far away.

If I've learned anything traveling the world, it's the power of hope. The power of one person — Washington, Lincoln, King, Mandela, even Malala from Pakistan — one person can change the world by giving people hope.

So, if you want to change the world, start singing when you're up to your neck in mud.
第九周被称为"地狱周"。六天不睡觉、持续骚扰，还有圣迭戈与蒂华纳之间泥滩的特殊一天——沼泽地形，泥浆会吞噬你。

地狱周的星期三，你在泥潭泡15小时，忍受冰冷刺骨的泥浆、呼啸的风和教官不断施压让你放弃。当太阳落山时，因为犯了严重违规，全班被命令进入泥潭。泥浆吞没了每个人，只剩下头露在外面。

教官说只要有5个人放弃就能出去。环顾四周，有人快要放弃了。距离日出还有八个多小时的刺骨寒冷。牙齿打颤和呻吟声大到几乎听不到任何东西。

然后一个声音开始在夜空中回荡——唱歌。完全走调，但唱得非常热情。一个人变两个，两个变三个，很快全班都在唱。教官威胁说继续唱就让我们待更久，但歌声依然持续。不知怎的泥潭似乎暖了一点，风没那么猛了，黎明不那么远了。

如果这些年我学到了一件事，那就是希望的力量。华盛顿、林肯、金博士、曼德拉、甚至巴基斯坦女孩马拉拉——一个人可以通过给别人希望来改变世界。

所以，当你身陷泥潭齐脖子深的时候，开始唱歌吧。
10Don't Ever Ring the Bell 🔔 · 永远不要敲响那面钟
Finally, in SEAL training there is a bell. A brass bell that hangs in the center of the compound for all the students to see. All you have to do to quit is ring the bell.

Ring the bell and you no longer have to wake up at 5 o'clock. Ring the bell and you no longer have to do freezing cold swims. Ring the bell and you no longer have to do runs, obstacle course, PT — and you no longer have to endure the hardships of training. Just ring the bell.

If you want to change the world, don't ever, ever ring the bell.
最后，海豹训练场上挂着一面黄铜钟。想退出？敲一下就行。

敲钟就不用5点起床，不用做冰冷的游泳，不用跑步、跑障碍、做体能训练——一切痛苦即刻结束。只要敲那面钟。

如果你想改变世界，绝对不要、永远不要敲响那面钟。

To the graduating class of 2014, you are moments away from graduating. Moments away from beginning your journey through life. Moments away from starting to change the world — for the better. It will not be easy.

But YOU are the class of 2014, the class that can affect the lives of 800 million people in the next century. Start each day with a task completed. Find someone to help you through life. Respect everyone. Know that life is not fair and that you will fail often.

But if you take some risks, step up when the times are toughest, face down the bullies, lift up the downtrodden and never, ever give up — if you do these things, then the next generation and the generations that follow will live in a world far better than the one we have today. And what started here will indeed have changed the world — for the better.

Thank you very much. Hook 'em horns.
2014届的毕业生们，你们即将毕业，即将开启人生旅程，即将开始让这个世界变得更好。这不会容易。

但你们是2014届——可以影响下一世纪8亿人的那一届。每天从完成一项任务开始。找人帮你走过人生。尊重每个人。知道生活不公平，你会经常失败。

但如果你敢于冒险，在最艰难的时刻挺身而出，面对霸凌者，扶起受压迫的人，永远永不放弃——那么下一代以及随后的几代人将生活在一个比今天更美好的世界中。从这里开始的，确实会改变世界——让它变得更美好。

非常感谢。Hook 'em horns（德克萨斯大学加油口号）！

Admiral William H. McRaven (Navy SEAL, 36 years)
University of Texas at Austin Commencement Address, May 17, 2014
